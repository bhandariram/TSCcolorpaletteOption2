Design Philosophy
A Warmer, Earth-Rooted Approach
While Option A leaned on institutional navy and crimson, this palette draws from the physical world of Tamang culture — clay temples, hand-dyed textiles, monsoon twilight, and terraced fields.

From the Land, For the People

The Tamang people are deeply connected to their landscape — the red-clay monasteries of Langtang, the indigo twilight over Kathmandu Valley, the amber glow of oil lamps during Lhosar celebrations, and the verdant terraces that cascade down Himalayan hillsides.

This alternative palette translates those tangible, sensory experiences into a digital colour system. It feels warmer and more organic than Option A, creating a sense of homecoming — especially important for a diaspora community thousands of kilometres from Nepal.

The combination of earthy maroon with spiritual indigo creates a unique identity that no other Canadian community organisation uses, making the Tamang Society instantly recognisable.

01

Earthy Warmth

Maroon and amber create an inviting, home-like atmosphere that encourages community participation — especially for members who may be less comfortable with digital platforms.

02

Spiritual Depth

Indigo introduces a contemplative, sacred dimension — connecting the digital platform to the meditative and spiritual traditions central to Tamang identity.

03

Distinct Identity

This combination is rare in Canadian community organisations. While many default to blue-red-white, this palette stands apart, making the society instantly memorable.

04

Warm Neutrals

Unlike Option A's cool grays, Option B uses warm-toned neutrals (cream, stone, clay) that harmonise with the earthy palette and feel more approachable.

Cultural Rationale
Why Each Color Suits the Organisation
Every colour maps to a specific facet of Tamang life, landscape, and community purpose.

Temple Maroon — Shelter & Roots

The deep red-brown of Tamang gompa walls and clay-brick monasteries across Langtang and Rasuwa. As the primary colour, it grounds the entire platform in a sense of place and belonging — something the diaspora community deeply craves. It communicates: "This is your home, even online."

#5E2A25

Indigo Dusk — Spirit & Contemplation

The deep purple-blue of twilight over the Kathmandu Valley, when monastery bells ring and butter lamps are lit. Indigo carries spiritual weight in Tamang Buddhist tradition. As the CTA colour, it invites thoughtful action — membership is not a transaction but a connection to something deeper.

#4E3FA0

Sunrise Amber — Joy & Celebration

The warm amber of sunrise over the Himalayas and the golden glow of oil lamps during Lhosar and Sonam Lhosar festivals. Warmer and more vivid than Option A's gold, this amber captures the celebratory, joyful side of Tamang culture. It highlights what matters: events, achievements, and milestones.

#E89B1C

Paddy Teal — Growth & Renewal

The blue-green of irrigated paddy terraces — the agricultural backbone of Tamang communities in Nepal. Teal conveys both growth and trustworthiness. In the WebApp, it signals positive outcomes: approved memberships, completed payments, and successful certificate generation. It feels fresher and more modern than a traditional green.

#2E9E89

Warm Stone — Comfort Across Generations

Unlike Option A's cool blue-grays, this palette uses warm cream-to-brown neutrals reminiscent of handmade Tamang paper and stone-walled homes. This warmth is not just aesthetic — it is functional. Research consistently shows that warm-toned interfaces feel more approachable and less intimidating, which matters enormously for a community WebApp used by elderly members, recent immigrants with varying English proficiency, and young families alike. The warm stone tones create a quiet visual harmony with the maroon and amber, ensuring the platform never feels cold or corporate.

Application Guide
Where Each Color Lives
Quick reference for the development team (S2 & S3) to apply Option B colours consistently.

Header, Footer & Hero

Maroon-600 background with white text. Top bar uses Maroon-700 for depth.

Primary CTA Buttons

Indigo gradient (500→300) with white text. Hover state shifts to 600→400.

Badges & Highlights

Amber-400 for featured badges and hover borders. Text on amber uses Amber-900.

Success & Approval States

Teal-400 for checkmarks, approved badges, and payment confirmation toasts.

Section Backgrounds

Alternating Snow/Mist sections. Cards use white with warm Border stroke.

Error & Warning States

Maroon-50 background with Maroon-700 text for form validation and alerts.

Info & Tooltip Backgrounds

Indigo-50 with Indigo-700 text for informational banners and status pills.

Typography System

Headings use warm Charcoal (#231E1A). Body uses warm Body (#706458).
